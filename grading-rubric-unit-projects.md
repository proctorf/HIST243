# Unit Projects Grading Rubric
**HIST 243: Sexuality in Latin America**

## Overall Grade Distribution
- **Historical Argument & Thesis (25%)**
- **Engagement with Readings (25%)**
- **Synthesis & Analysis (20%)**
- **Source Use & Evidence (15%)**
- **Format & Presentation (10%)**
- **Reflection (Nontraditional formats only) (5%)**

---

## 1. Historical Argument & Thesis (25 points)

### Excellent (23-25 points)
- Presents a clear, sophisticated, and defendable thesis about sexuality as a social/historical construct
- Argument demonstrates nuanced understanding of power relations, context, and historiographical significance
- Thesis is consistently developed and supported throughout the project
- Shows mastery of historical thinking and argumentation

### Good (20-22 points)
- Presents a clear and defendable thesis with good historical grounding
- Shows solid understanding of sexuality as a historical construct
- Argument is generally well-developed with minor gaps in consistency
- Demonstrates competent historical thinking

### Satisfactory (17-19 points)
- Thesis is present but may lack clarity or sophistication
- Basic understanding of historical argumentation evident
- Some development of argument throughout project
- Meets minimum expectations for historical analysis

### Needs Improvement (0-16 points)
- Thesis is unclear, absent, or not historically grounded
- Limited understanding of historical argumentation
- Argument lacks development or coherence
- Falls below expectations for college-level historical analysis

---

## 2. Engagement with Readings (25 points)

### Excellent (23-25 points)
- Demonstrates deep, critical engagement with 3-5+ required readings
- Uses direct quotations and paraphrases effectively and precisely
- Shows sophisticated understanding of each source's arguments and perspectives
- Addresses historiographical debates and different interpretive approaches
- Goes beyond surface-level engagement to analyze authors' methodologies and assumptions

### Good (20-22 points)
- Engages meaningfully with 3-5 required readings
- Uses quotations and paraphrases appropriately
- Shows good understanding of source arguments
- Some attention to historiographical context
- Demonstrates close reading skills

### Satisfactory (17-19 points)
- Uses required number of readings (3-5) but engagement may be shallow
- Basic use of quotations and paraphrases
- Shows general understanding of source content
- Limited attention to historiographical debates
- Meets minimum engagement expectations

### Needs Improvement (0-16 points)
- Uses fewer than required readings or shows minimal engagement
- Limited or inappropriate use of evidence from sources
- Misunderstands key arguments from readings
- No attention to historiographical context
- Falls below minimum engagement standards

---

## 3. Synthesis & Analysis (20 points)

### Excellent (18-20 points)
- Skillfully synthesizes multiple perspectives to build coherent argument
- Identifies and analyzes agreements, tensions, and contradictions between sources
- Moves significantly beyond summary to offer original analysis
- Shows how different sources illuminate power relations and everyday practices
- Demonstrates sophisticated critical thinking

### Good (16-17 points)
- Generally synthesizes sources effectively
- Identifies some connections and tensions between readings
- Goes beyond summary with solid analysis
- Shows understanding of broader implications
- Demonstrates good critical thinking skills

### Satisfactory (14-15 points)
- Basic synthesis of sources with some analysis
- Limited identification of connections between readings
- Some movement beyond summary
- Shows general understanding of historical significance
- Meets basic expectations for analysis

### Needs Improvement (0-13 points)
- Relies primarily on summary rather than synthesis
- Little identification of connections between sources
- Limited analysis or critical thinking
- Fails to demonstrate understanding of broader significance
- Below expectations for college-level work

---

## 4. Source Use & Evidence (15 points)

### Excellent (14-15 points)
- Uses precise, well-contextualized evidence (quotations and paraphrases)
- Excellent integration of evidence to support argument
- Proper citations in Chicago or MLA style with complete bibliography
- Evidence is effectively contextualized within historical moments
- Shows attention to source reliability and perspective

### Good (12-13 points)
- Uses appropriate evidence to support argument
- Good integration of quotations and paraphrases
- Generally proper citations and bibliography with minor errors
- Evidence is adequately contextualized
- Shows some attention to source perspective

### Satisfactory (11 points)
- Basic use of evidence with adequate integration
- Citations present but may have formatting issues
- Evidence supports argument but contextualization may be limited
- Bibliography present but may be incomplete
- Meets minimum citation requirements

### Needs Improvement (0-10 points)
- Limited or ineffective use of evidence
- Poor integration of quotations and paraphrases
- Significant citation errors or missing bibliography
- Little contextualization of evidence
- Falls below academic standards for source use

---

## 5. Format & Presentation (10 points)

### For Traditional Essays:
- **Excellent (9-10):** Meets length requirements (6+ pages), excellent organization, clear prose, error-free
- **Good (8):** Generally meets requirements, well-organized, mostly clear writing, few errors
- **Satisfactory (7):** Meets basic requirements, adequate organization, some clarity issues or errors
- **Needs Improvement (0-6):** Below length requirements, poor organization, unclear writing, many errors

### For Nontraditional Formats:
- **Excellent (9-10):** Creative and effective use of format, professional presentation, clear navigation/flow
- **Good (8):** Good use of format, solid presentation quality, easy to follow
- **Satisfactory (7):** Adequate use of format, basic presentation, generally accessible
- **Needs Improvement (0-6):** Poor use of format, unprofessional presentation, difficult to navigate

---

## 6. Reflection (Nontraditional formats only) (5 points)

### Excellent (5 points)
- Thoughtful, detailed reflection on how format influenced argument development
- Clear explanation of how format affected source selection and integration
- Demonstrates understanding of relationship between form and content
- Shows critical thinking about methodological choices

### Good (4 points)
- Solid reflection on format's influence on argument
- Good explanation of impact on source use
- Shows awareness of form/content relationship
- Demonstrates some critical thinking about choices

### Satisfactory (3 points)
- Basic reflection present but may lack depth
- Limited explanation of format's impact
- Shows minimal awareness of methodological considerations
- Meets minimum reflection requirement

### Needs Improvement (0-2 points)
- Reflection absent, incomplete, or superficial
- No clear explanation of format's influence
- Lacks critical thinking about methodological choices
- Falls below minimum standards

---

## Additional Considerations

### Late Submission Policy
- Projects submitted 1-3 days late: -5 points
- Projects submitted 4-7 days late: -10 points
- Projects submitted more than 1 week late: -20 points

### Format-Specific Guidelines
Each nontraditional format should meet the specific requirements outlined in the assignment description (page counts, technical specifications, etc.). Failure to meet basic format requirements will result in point deductions from the Format & Presentation category.

### Academic Integrity
All projects must represent original work. Plagiarism or other forms of academic dishonesty will result in failure of the assignment and may result in failure of the course.

---

## Grade Scale
- A: 90-100 points (Excellent work demonstrating mastery)
- B: 80-89 points (Good work meeting most expectations)
- C: 70-79 points (Satisfactory work meeting basic expectations)
- D: 60-69 points (Below expectations, significant improvement needed)
- F: Below 60 points (Failing to meet minimum standards)

This rubric emphasizes historical thinking skills while accommodating creative approaches to presenting historical arguments. Students should strive to demonstrate their understanding of sexuality as a complex social and historical construct through evidence-based analysis, regardless of their chosen format.
